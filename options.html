Black List

Black list allow your Pavlok to zap, vibrate and beep if you visit some site you want to avoid.

For instance: "www.facebook.com" might be on your blacklist. If you visit the site, you'll get zapped.

For listing sites, type in the address and hit Enter or click Save.

Please choose your black sites, separated by commas:

Exception List

Exception addresses allow you to use a part of some site without triggering stimuli.

For instance: "www.amazon.com" might be on your blacklist. But you want to use "www.amazon.com/music" without being shocked.

Just put the address there, and it will ignore that specific address, but leave the rest of domain active as blacklisted.

Please tell your exceptions for black sites, separated by commas.

About extension

This extension is all about getting you on track to your habit goals.

If you struggle to keep distraction at bay when near the computer, this comes to rescue. There will be clear stakes here, as you'll be simultaneously made aware of the bad habit (and to a fix!) and get uncomfortable stimuli to assotiate with the bad habit.

BlackList reasoning

When you get on a Blacklisted site, you'll be notified about it and prompted to change your behavior (aka "Get the hell outta here!"). This will remind you to think why are you going there and decide if it's worth doing it. To help you ponder, the extension adds a zap every 5 seconds you stay in the bad bad bad sites.

It gets way easier to stick and change your behavior when consequences are real AND you get called in real time about the bad behavior, with a chance to fix it. You'll soon get rid of bad browsing habits with it!

There are also some advanced options, like Whitelist. It came in because sometimes there are gems amidst the dirty. For instance, there should be no problem is listening to music using Amazon, but you don't want to loosen your wallet nor your evening browsing it's seductive shop. So you can Blacklist amazon.com, but create an exception (whitelist) for amazon.com/music

Tab limit reasoning

It will also help you look at the messy infinite number of open tabs in a more concious way. You set a limit and it will tell you whenever you start to get close or over the limit. If the simple mention of the limit isn't enough to make you ponder why so many tabs, there will be a zap to make it more enlightening. Soon there will be no more 1264376 tabs open and a slow pc because of it!

If you want to make a crash change to your behavior, it can be sped up using the zap on close option. Pavlok won't zap you when you adjust course (closing excess tabs, for instance). However, it's possible to make yourself way more sensible to the tab numbers by adding more stimulus. This option will make you remember TWICE not to open excess tabs. Not necessary, but sure speed breaking the tabs hoarding habit.
